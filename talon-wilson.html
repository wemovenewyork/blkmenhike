At 42 years old, Talon Wilson has become a respected voice and inspiring presence in the growing community of African American male hikers.

Known for his fearless approach to both life and the trail, Wilson's journey into hiking wasn't just about exploring nature — it was about reclaiming space, redefining identity, and embracing freedom in its purest form.

Born and raised in Queens, New York, Wilson spent much of his early life surrounded by concrete and city noise. Nature, at the time, felt distant — almost inaccessible. It wasn't until his early 30s, after a period of personal and professional burnout, that he stumbled upon hiking during a weekend trip to the Catskills. That first hike changed everything.

"
It felt like exhaling for the first time in years.

What began as a casual escape quickly transformed into a lifestyle. Over the past decade, Wilson has tackled trails across the country — from the rugged terrains of the Appalachian Mountains to the serene beauty of the Pacific Northwest. His hiking philosophy centers on presence, self-awareness, and authenticity.

Wilson's approach to the trail runs deeper than fitness or scenery. For him, stepping into the wilderness is about stripping away the noise — expectations, fear, and the weight of other people's perceptions. "Out on the trail, you're just you," he explains. "No titles, no noise, just truth."

"
Out on the trail, you're just you — no titles, no noise, just truth.

Wilson has also become an advocate for increasing representation in outdoor spaces. Through social media and community outreach, he encourages more Black men to explore hiking, emphasizing both the physical and mental health benefits. He frequently organizes group hikes aimed at beginners, creating welcoming environments for those who may feel intimidated stepping into outdoor culture.

His impact goes beyond the trail. Wilson speaks openly about mental health, vulnerability, and the importance of finding stillness in a fast-paced world. "Hiking saved me," he says candidly. "It gave me clarity, discipline, and peace."

"
Hiking saved me. It gave me clarity, discipline, and peace.

Today, Talon Wilson is more than just an avid hiker — he's a movement. A reminder that the outdoors belongs to everyone, and that sometimes, the greatest journey you can take is the one that leads you back to yourself.
