NYC CHAPTER 17 CROSSINGS CHAPTER ELDER

Talon Wilson came up in Queens. He found the trail at 32 during the worst year of his life, and he never left. Now he's the man other men call when they need a big brother — on the mountain and off it.

The year Talon Wilson first hiked, he had just lost his father, his job, and what he describes as "the version of myself I'd been performing for 32 years." It was not a good year for anyone around him. He stopped returning calls. He stopped going out. He was in the city of eight million people and completely alone with it.

A colleague — not a close friend, just a man he knew from work — invited him on a day hike in the Catskills. Talon went because declining felt like more work than going. He drove two hours, walked six miles, said almost nothing the entire time, and cried on the descent. Not the kind of crying that needs an explanation. The kind that happens when a body finally has enough space to release what it's been holding.

"
I drove home and I couldn't tell you anything about the trail. I just knew I needed to go back.

He went back. Then he went again. Within six months he had joined BMH NYC and was doing crossings with brothers he'd never met — men who were strangers until they weren't, until the trail made them something else. Talon crossed Breakneck Ridge in October of that year. It was raining. The rock scramble was wet and cold and legitimately dangerous in sections. He made it with a brother named DeShawn who was also on his first crossing and also not saying much and also clearly needed to be there.

Talon and DeShawn have hiked together 23 times since that day. DeShawn is his little brother in the chapter. Talon brought him in, walked his first serious trail beside him, answered his texts at midnight when the weight got heavy. That's the pattern that repeats — men who were helped by the trail reaching back to bring the next one in.

"
The man who brought me to that first hike didn't know he was saving my life. I try to remember that every time I invite someone new.

Talon is now a chapter elder in BMH NYC — the informal designation for brothers who have crossed 15 or more trails and have brought in multiple little brothers. He helps lead crossings, runs the pre-hike logistics for new members, and is the first call the chapter lead makes when a brother is going through something hard.

He is not an influencer. He has no hiking social media. He does not post his miles. What he does is show up — every weekend, in every season, for brothers who are where he was in the worst year of his life and need someone to say: come. There's a trail. You need to be on it. I'll be there.
